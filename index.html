About Me

I am a current PhD student at the University of California, Santa Cruz, and received my B.Sc. in physics from Northeastern University in 2021. I am broadly interested in instrumentation and exoplanet high-contrast imaging, and am part of the SCALES (Slicer Combined with an Array of Lenslets for Exoplanet Spectroscopy) instrument team. Outside of research, I am frequently sunburnt and out of breath somewhere remote, and I've been a competitive fencer for 15 years.

Mentorship & outreach

I deeply believe that science must be accessible and inclusive, which takes active and thoughful work to make happen. I spend time on general astronomy outreach to the public, mentoring physics & astronomy undergraduate students, and cultivating positive work dynamics within my home department.

Mentorship:

I support three UCSC physics undergraduate students. In weekly individual meetings, we connect over the lived experience of being a woman in physics, and I try to provide them with the support and connections they need to create their own success.

Public outreach:

Science is at its most valuable when it is shared in an understandable, empowering way with non-experts. I aim to give one public talk per quarter about general astronomy and my research. I've previously been hosted by local elementary, middle, and high schools, and local Astronomy on Tap events. I also respond to public questions via Lick Observatory's AskAnAstronomer.
